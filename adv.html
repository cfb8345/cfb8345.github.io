Associate Verification Webpage

Background

As an intern working my first tech role on a team that served to create and maintain mostly backend processes and applications, an opportunity arose to design and prototype a simple site dedicated to ensuring all company employees received their associate discount.

Since this simple page would be used by a wide range of people (teens to elderly, no technological background to the senior developers in the support center) and was required to be completed rather quickly, it was made to be a simple form with modals that confirmed input information was what the user intended.

Process

While I may have been tasked with the design and prototyping of the site, the main web developer on the team and the team management had final say in the appearance overall. Lobbying for better UX was something that had to be done often when working on projects with the team, as there was no dedicated UX team or teammate within the company.

The first phase was trying to get an idea of what the form will generally look like and how the feedback will be given for each interaction (loading after clicking submit, error feedback). As the project went on, the team received sporadic requirements, and for each requirement, a new phase of design and prototyping was started.

For the second phase, modals were introduced as confirmation of input information and some fields in the form were removed for clarification.

The last phase added some variation in the modals and more total sections overall. The final version of the site has since been taken down, as it was meant to be a short tenure to help clean up employees who were severed but were still receiving their discount. Overall, I was happy with the way this webpage turned out, as it was my first design that would actually be used by many real people in a real-time context. It was incredibly exciting to know that this webpage would be used by nearly 20,000 employees and to know by the end of my time with Weis Markets that it was a successful verification process.
